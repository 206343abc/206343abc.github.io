Core Journaling Prompts

Use these structured prompts to move beyond surface-level feelings. Writing helps to organize thoughts, reduce stress, and gain new perspectives on challenging situations.

Cognitive Reframing

Identify one negative, automatic thought you had today (e.g., "I'm going to fail this"). Write it down, then challenge it. What is a more realistic, compassionate alternative view?

The 'Feeling Map'

What was the strongest emotion you felt today? Describe it. Where did you feel it in your body? What event or thought triggered it? Just observe, don't judge.

Detailed Gratitude

List three *small, specific* things you are grateful for right now (e.g., "the taste of my coffee," "a 5-minute quiet moment"). Why did this specific thing matter?

Future Self Visualization

Describe your 'future self' one month from now, having successfully navigated a current challenge. What one small step did you take today to start moving in that direction?

Advanced Journaling Techniques

Ready to go deeper? These techniques are designed for specific outcomes, from releasing intense emotion to solving complex problems.

The 10-Minute 'Worry Dump'

Set a timer for 10 minutes. For that entire time, write *everything* that is worrying you, big or small, rational or irrational. Do not stop writing. When the timer goes off, close the notebook. This externalizes anxiety and stops it from looping in your head.

The 'Unsent Letter'

Are you feeling strong emotions (like anger or sadness) towards a person or situation? Write a letter expressing *everything* you feel, uncensored. Do not send it. The purpose is release, not communication. Read it, then delete or destroy it.

Core Manual Techniques

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Method

Use this when you feel a panic attack or intense anxiety. It forces your brain to focus on the present moment. Look around and name:

  • 5 Things you can SEE
  • 4 Things you can TOUCH
  • 3 Things you can HEAR
  • 2 Things you can SMELL
  • 1 Thing you can TASTE

Square (Box) Breathing

A simple, powerful rhythmic breathing technique to calm the nervous system.

  • Inhale slowly for a count of 4.
  • Hold your breath for a count of 4.
  • Exhale slowly for a count of 4.
  • Hold the exhale for a count of 4.
